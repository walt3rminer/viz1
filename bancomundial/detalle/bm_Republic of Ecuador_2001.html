 25200000 PROLOCAL's development objective is to support local empowerment, improve quality of local services, and increase access to productive assets by the rural poor in six poor micro-regions of the country. To address these issues, PROLOCAL emphasizes the importance of (i) social capital development based on the organization, (ii) human capital formation based on education and services, and (iii) access to productive assets based on sustainable use of natural resources, value-added chains, and off-farm economic opportunities linked to peri-urban activity. PROLOCAL approach of using communities and their territorial or second-tier organizations to implement the project follows the arrangements being carried out by the Bank-financed Indigenous and Afro-Ecuadorian Development Project (PRODEPINE).
