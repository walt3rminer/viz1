0 The proposed project will contribute to the global goal of improved agrobiodiversity conservation by focusing on three objectives specific to Central America: ·,,Application of genetic diversity knowledge for targeting wild relatives of crops in the Mesoamerican Corridor within overall conservation efforts; ·,,Capacity building and promotion of policies fostering agrobiodversity conservation in the region; and ·,,Increasing potential benefits to farmers and rural communities from integrating biodiversity conservation in their agriculture practices and sustainable use of natural resources. The project will support participating countries in implementing two important international treaties: the Conservation on Biological Diversity and the International Treaty on Plant Genetic Resources for Food and Agriculture. This will be done through applied research using recent advances in genomic and geographic sciences and though the promotion of policies and capacity building for the implementation of integrated conservation of wild relatives of crops. This project is being dropped by the Sector Manager on behalf of the TTL, Nadim Khouri, who is no longer in the Region.
