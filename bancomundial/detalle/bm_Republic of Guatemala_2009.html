The overall objective of the project is to assist Guatemala put in place measures in the energy sector that will allow it to reduce its dependence on imported oil. The project would employ both short- and longer-term options involving both investment and technical assistance. Among the short-term options are: (i) increased regional trade, (ii) power system management improvements to reduce losses, (iii) operation and maintenance measures to increase load factors, and (iv) demand-side management (energy efficiency) measures for residential, commercial, industrial, and agricultural sectors. Among the longer-term options are: (i) repowering of existing power capacity, such as hydropower rehabilitation and high-efficiency bagasse cogeneration, (ii) transmission investments, both domestic and regional, and (iii) investment in new generation capacity, including coal, hydro, LNG, geothermal and other renewables. Technical assistance will also be included, such as: (i) power system planning to promote least-cost investments, including capital-intensive, (ii) policy measures to enhance the use of domestic and renewable energy resources, and (iii) regulatory measures to attract private sector investment within the context of a national plan.
