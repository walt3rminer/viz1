 19000000 DON'T PUB DROPPED The development objective of the project is to minimize the threat posed to humans by HPAI infection and other zoonoses and to prepare for, control, and respond to influenza pandemics and other infectious disease emergencies in humans. To achieve this, the project would focus on three areas: prevention, preparedness and planning; and, response and containment. Short-and long-term actions need to be taken in parallel, and efforts should be made to ensure that short-term responses are consistent with and contribute to proposed longer-term interventions. By concentrating the efforts on these, dual-use investments would generate benefits during normal times and in a pandemic. The project would finance activities under four components: (i) animal health, (ii) human health, (iii) public awareness and information, and (iv) implementation support, monitoring and evaluation. Even though the activities are organized by sectoral components, the project would promote and strongly endorse development and integration of integrated national plans that ensure common objectives across sectors for dealing with this issue. Preliminary estimates of project costs are in the range of $15-20m. About $3m could come from the AHITF (maybe more if a specific country case can be made). The remainder could potentially come from the GPAI, but these funds would have to come from within the existing country IDA envelop - unless a specific country case can be made for additionality. As this initiative is regarded as an emergency operation, it was always assumed that the project should be prepared and appraised as soon as possible and that funding sources would be finalized in parallel - using a well prepared and appraised project as a vehicle to attract funding from wherever it is available

 25000000 The development objective of the Balochistan Small Scale Irrigation Project (BSSIP) is to support efforts by the GoBalochistan to improve the management of scarce water resources in the Pishin Lora Basin (PLB) by reducing the overall impact of the present water crisis. Project activities are designed to recognize the importance of direct participation of water users and other stakeholders.
