 0 Task Description The project would purchase ERs generated by the replacement of about 9 to 10 million ILs with CFLs during 2009-10 in 10 urban and rural areas with the largest population concentration in Bangladesh at the rate of 4 CFLs per household. The plan is to install an additional 15 to 20 million CFLs in the subsequent phase. The purchase will be made by the World Bank managed Spanish Carbon Fund (SCF). Infrastructure Development Company Limited (IDCOL) would be the coordinating entity and the bundler for the project. The imcome earned from the sale of the ERs will be placed in a renewable energy fund, managed by IDCOL, and will be used to finance variousenergy efficiency project. Introduction of CFLs on a large scale will reduce demand for electricity and decrease the use of fossil fuels. This will have a positive impact on local air quality by reducing pollutants generated by fossil fuel based power. Reduced electricity bills will increase disposable income. Since CFL bulbs last longer, it will also reduce household expenciture on purchasing lamps. In the short term, the deployment of CFLs on such a large scale will create employment opportunities, institutional development through the enhancement of program management skills and training, and increase local knowledge of energy efficiency.
